Accounting and Finance Report

This report was prepared for the Fundamentals of Accounting and Finance course as part of a business certificate program during my undergraduate education. The course presents and introduction to topics in accounting and cooperate finance. For the accounting segment students are required to read and analyze financial statements including income statement, balance sheets and statement of cashflows. The finance part introduces how to value investment opportunities and make financial projections. As a part of this course, students are required to write two reports during each part of the course. One of the two reports is presented below.

The paper required analysis, calculations and interpretations of Intel’s 2018 financial statements. Preparation of the paper was done as a team of three students. The report mainly focuses on commenting on Intel’s liquidity, solvency and profitability metrics in order to determine the health of the company. To do this Intel’s performance was compared between 2017 and 2018 as well as compared to AMD, an industry competitor. Many metrics were sorted out from numerous financial statements and necessary calculations were done in the appendices to support all claims.

This assignment has allowed me to gain an important skill of being about to evaluate a company’s performance. Financial literacy is important in the engineering field as it helps with understanding project and company decision making in the workplace. Being able to use the provided data and performing calculations to produce meaningful numbers for comparison is another engineering skill exemplified in this report. In addition, working in yet another team environment provided essential experience for future work in the engineering field.
